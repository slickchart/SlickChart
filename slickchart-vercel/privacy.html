Privacy Policy

SlickChart · Effective date: July 10, 2026

Who we are: SlickChart (“SlickChart,” “we,” “us”) is a practice-management app operated by SlickChart, LLC, a California limited liability company, Pleasant Hill, CA. Questions: support@slickchart.app.

1. Overview

SlickChart helps beauty and wellness professionals (“Providers”) manage their practice — client charts, appointments, forms, photos, product recommendations, messaging, and payments. This policy explains what we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

There are two kinds of people in SlickChart, and it matters which one you are:

  • Providers — the professionals who sign up for and use SlickChart.
  • Clients — the customers of those Providers, whose information a Provider enters into SlickChart.

For information a Provider enters about their Clients, the Provider is responsible for that information and for having the right to store it (see Section 6). SlickChart processes it on the Provider’s behalf.

2. Information we collect

  • a. Provider account information. Your name, email address, a securely hashed password, business details you enter (business name, hours, branding), and language preference.
  • b. Client information you (the Provider) enter. This may include a Client’s name, contact details, birthday, appointment history, treatment notes, intake-form answers, and — depending on how you use SlickChart — skin/health details, allergies, contraindications, medications you note, and photos. You control what you enter.
  • c. Payment information. When you take Client payments or (in the future) pay for a SlickChart subscription, card details are entered and processed by Square on their secure systems. SlickChart never sees, handles, or stores full card numbers.
  • d. Usage and technical information. Basic technical data needed to run the app (for example, log data and error reports). We do not sell this.
  • e. Feedback. If you submit beta feedback, we store your message and rating (and your account email) so we can improve the product.

3. How we use information

We use information to: provide and operate the app; authenticate your account; sync your Square data when you connect it; send transactional emails (verification, password resets); send product updates if you opt in; respond to feedback and support; keep the service secure; and comply with law.

We do not sell your information or your Clients’ information. We do not use Client health-adjacent information for advertising.

4. AI features

Some SlickChart features can use AI to draft suggestions (for example, note or message drafts). These features are optional and can be turned off in Settings. When used, the relevant text is sent to our AI provider to generate a response. Do not rely on AI output as medical advice.

5. Service providers (subprocessors)

We use trusted companies to run SlickChart. Each receives only what it needs:

  • Vercel — application hosting.
  • Neon — the database that stores your account and the data you enter.
  • Square — payment processing and, if you connect it, syncing your clients/appointments/catalog.
  • Resend — sending transactional and (opt-in) newsletter emails.
  • our third-party AI provider — generating optional AI suggestions when you use those features.

We will keep this list current. If we add a subprocessor that materially changes how data is handled, we’ll update this policy.

6. Providers’ responsibilities for Client data

If you are a Provider, you are responsible for:

  • Having the legal right and any necessary consent to store your Clients’ information and photos in SlickChart.
  • Telling your Clients, as required by law, how their information is used.
  • Keeping your login secure and only entering information you’re permitted to.

You are the party that decides what Client information to collect and why. SlickChart acts as your service provider for that data.

7. How we protect information

We use reasonable safeguards, including encryption of data in transit, hashed passwords, and encryption of connected Square access tokens. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information and will notify you of a breach as required by law.

8. Data retention and deletion

We keep information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the service. You can ask us to delete your account and associated data by emailing support@slickchart.app; we will delete or de-identify it within a reasonable period, except where we must keep certain records to comply with law. Some information may remain in backups for a limited time.

9. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, delete, or receive a copy of your personal information, and to opt out of marketing. To exercise these rights, email support@slickchart.app.

California residents: Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (as amended), you have rights to know, delete, correct, and opt out of “sharing” of personal information. We do not sell personal information. To make a request, contact us at the email above; we will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

10. Email preferences

Transactional emails (verification, password resets) are required to use the service. Marketing/product-update emails are opt-in and every one includes an unsubscribe link.

11. Children

SlickChart is for professionals and is not directed to children under 18. Do not create an account if you are under 18. Providers are responsible for any legal requirements that apply when a Client is a minor.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy. If we make material changes, we’ll update the effective date and, where appropriate, notify you in the app or by email.
